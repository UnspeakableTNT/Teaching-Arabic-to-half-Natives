Learn About ه (Heh) and ة (Taa Marbuta)

Appearance of ه (Heh)

Beginning: هـ (e.g., هنا - Huna)

Middle: ـهـ (e.g., بهجة - Bahja)

End: ـه (e.g., سماه - Samah)

Taa Marbuta (ة)

Taa Marbuta (ة) is a special form of 'Heh' used at the end of some feminine nouns and adjectives. It looks like a 'Heh' with two dots on top. It is pronounced like a regular 'Heh' (ه).

For example: مدرسة (Madrasah - school), ساعة (Saa'ah - hour)

Pronunciation of ه (Heh) and ة (Taa Marbuta)

The letter ه (Heh) and ة (Taa Marbuta) are pronounced as 'h' in 'happy'. It's a voiceless glottal fricative sound.
